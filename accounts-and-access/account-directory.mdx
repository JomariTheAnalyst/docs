## What each record must include

- Platform or account name
- Business login email or account handle
- Business purpose
- Business owner or accountable role
- Person responsible for the password
- Person responsible for MFA
- Who can grant or remove access
- Recovery method and custodian
- Signed-in company devices
- Last verification status

Use `To verify` when an external dashboard or authorized owner has not confirmed a fact. Do not infer ownership from the person who uses an account most often.

## Grant access

1. Record the person's business email, required task, platform, minimum role, and access duration.
2. Obtain approval from the business owner or authorized platform administrator.
3. Use a named account when the platform supports individual users.
4. Use the Business Owner Account only for approved owner-level, recovery, billing, security, or emergency work.
5. Confirm that the person can complete the approved task without reaching unrelated billing, security, customer, or owner functions.

## Handle passwords and recovery

The password, MFA seed, backup code, API token, and recovery key belong in the approved password manager or platform recovery process. Mintlify may identify the custodian and vault item name, but it must not contain the secret itself.

```text
Company Password Manager -> SixthGear -> [Verified vault item]
```

<Warning>
  Never add a masked password, authenticator QR code, one-time code, recovery code, private key, or API token to this directory.
</Warning>

## Review and remove access

Review account owners, administrators, pending invitations, shared mailboxes, connected applications, MFA methods, recovery contacts, active sessions, and signed-in devices after staffing changes or suspected exposure.

During offboarding:

1. Transfer approved business records and ownership.
2. Revoke platform membership, repository access, email delegation, documentation access, active sessions, and device authorization.
3. Rotate any shared credential the departing person could retrieve.
4. Confirm that billing, MFA, recovery, and support responsibility remain with an approved person.
5. Verify that the person can no longer access SixthGear systems.

<Check>
  An access change is complete when the approved person can perform the required work, unnecessary access is removed, and SixthGear retains a tested owner and recovery route.
</Check>
