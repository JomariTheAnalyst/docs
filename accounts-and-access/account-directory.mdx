The Account Directory is Sixthgear's source of truth for who handles each business account and where the account is used. It links the detailed social, email, website-platform, and device records without publishing credentials.

<Note>
This directory records who handles each account and where it is used.
Actual passwords are not written in the documentation.
</Note>

## What each directory records

- Account or platform name
- Login email or account handle
- Business purpose
- Who monitors messages or activity
- **Password handled by**
- Who can grant access
- Whether <Tooltip tip="Multifactor authentication requires another proof of identity in addition to a password.">MFA</Tooltip> is required
- Which company devices are signed in

Use `To verify` when an external dashboard or authorized owner has not confirmed a fact. Do not infer ownership from the person who uses an account most often.

## Grant and protect access

An *access request* is a recorded request that identifies who needs access, the business reason, the minimum role, the required duration, and the approver.

1. Record the person's business email, task, platform, minimum role, start date, and expiry date when access is temporary.
2. Have the business approver confirm the need and an authorized platform administrator select the least-privileged role.
3. Invite the person's named account when the platform supports individual users.
4. Require MFA for privileged access and confirm a recovery route.
5. Verify that the user can complete the approved task without reaching unrelated owner, billing, security, or customer functions.

## Handle passwords and recovery safely

The actual password, MFA seed, backup code, API token, and recovery key belong only in the approved password manager. Documentation may record a safe location such as:

```text
Company Password Manager -> Sixthgear -> [Vault item name]
```

The person or role in **Password handled by** is responsible for controlled retrieval, rotation, and recovery. It does not mean the password is copied into the directory or shared through chat.

<Warning>
Never create a masked-password field. It still encourages credentials to be tracked in the wrong place and does not identify who is responsible for the account.
</Warning>

## Review and remove access

Review owners, administrators, pending invitations, shared mailboxes, connected applications, service accounts, MFA methods, recovery contacts, active sessions, and signed-in devices after staffing changes or suspected exposure.

During offboarding:

1. Transfer approved business records and ownership.
2. Revoke platform membership, email delegation, repository access, documentation access, active sessions, and device authorization.
3. Rotate any shared credential the departing person could retrieve.
4. Confirm that billing, MFA, recovery, and support responsibility remain with an approved person.
5. Verify the person can no longer access Sixthgear systems.

<Check>
An access change is complete when the approved person can perform the required work, unnecessary access is removed, and Sixthgear still has a tested owner and recovery route.
</Check>
