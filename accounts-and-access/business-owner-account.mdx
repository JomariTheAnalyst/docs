## When to use this account

Use the business owner account for approved owner-level work, including account recovery, billing, user administration, security settings, and emergency access. It may also be used during the initial executive handoff when the correct platform role has not yet been assigned to a named account.

Use a named personal or business account for routine work when the platform supports individual users. This keeps activity traceable and makes future access changes safer.

## MFA approval process

1. Start the sign-in from the official platform page.
2. Contact Jomari Roco only after the platform requests MFA.
3. State your name, the platform, and the approximate request time.
4. Jomari verifies the request before approving it or providing the one-time code.
5. Complete the sign-in and confirm that the correct company workspace opened.
6. Report any MFA request that you did not initiate.

<Warning>
  Jomari Roco is currently the only documented MFA custodian. Assign a company-approved backup custodian or recovery method before treating the access handoff as complete.
</Warning>

## Recovery and access problems

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The password is unavailable">
    Confirm the approved password-manager item and its access permissions. Do not reset the password until the business owner or authorized administrator approves the change.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="MFA approval is unavailable">
    Stop the sign-in attempt and use the verified recovery process for that platform. The backup custodian and recovery-code location are currently marked **To assign** or **To verify**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The wrong company workspace opens">
    Sign out without making changes. Confirm the business email, platform account, store, project, or repository with the responsible administrator before signing in again.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="An unexpected MFA request appears">
    Deny or ignore the request, inform Jomari Roco, review active sessions, and change the password through the approved recovery process if unauthorized access is suspected.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
