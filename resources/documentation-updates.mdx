Use this page when a platform, interface, process, responsibility, product recommendation, price, or source changes. Documentation should change with the operating process so staff do not follow an outdated instruction.

## Update process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify the affected guidance">
    Find every page, navigation entry, internal link, screenshot, table, and recommendation affected by the change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the new information">
    Check repository behavior in source and external settings in the authoritative dashboard. Use dated official or reputable sources for time-sensitive products and prices.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit without exposing private information">
    Do not add passwords, tokens, customer data, payment details, recovery codes, or unnecessary personal information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the result">
    Confirm that the instruction is clear for its intended reader, links point to current pages, and transferred content remains available after restructuring.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validate and publish">
    Run Mintlify build, link, and accessibility checks. Publish through the approved Git review process so history records what changed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Information to record

- What changed and why
- Affected page paths
- Source or dashboard used for verification
- Research date for prices, compatibility, and availability
- Reviewer or approver when the change affects business operations
- Remaining facts that still require confirmation

<Note>
Git history is the documentation change record. Do not add a second table that becomes stale unless management establishes a maintained review process and owner.
</Note>
