## Purpose

Use rollback when the current deployment causes a production incident and restoring the last known-good release is safer than an immediate forward fix.

<Warning>
Confirm the exact Vercel rollback control with an authorized administrator before an incident. The repository does not verify a CLI rollback command.
</Warning>

## Roll back safely

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the incident">
    Record symptoms, affected routes, start time, deployment, customer impact, and external service status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the known-good deployment">
    Use prior verification records and smoke-test results. Do not choose only by timestamp.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preserve evidence">
    Keep relevant Vercel, GitHub, storefront, Shopify, Sanity, Strapi, Redis, and DNS evidence without exposing sensitive data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restore the deployment">
    Use the verified Vercel rollback or redeploy workflow. Record the approver and target deployment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify critical behavior">
    Check `/ph`, store, collection, product, search, cart, checkout handoff, CMS content, contact, and smoke tests.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close and follow up">
    Record recovery time, cause, restored deployment, validation, remaining risk, and the owner of the permanent fix.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Content and data caution

A code rollback does not automatically reverse Shopify, Sanity, Strapi, DNS, or environment changes. Identify and handle those changes separately with their platform owners.
