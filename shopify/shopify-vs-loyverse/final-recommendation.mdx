## Recommended decision

Sixthgear should keep Shopify as its primary commerce platform and move physical-store selling to Shopify POS.

Loyverse should be retired after the replacement in-store workflow is working and its required historical data has been preserved.

<Check>
Keep Shopify, introduce Shopify POS for the physical store, and treat Loyverse as a temporary transition system rather than the long-term POS platform.
</Check>

## Why this is the best fit

- Shopify already supports the online store.
- The Next.js storefront already depends on Shopify.
- One platform reduces duplicate product and inventory work.
- Products, inventory, customers, and orders remain in one environment.
- Fewer integrations need monitoring and recovery.
- Long-term storefront and POS development is simpler.
- Removing Loyverse changes the in-store workflow without replacing online commerce.
- Removing Shopify would replace the ecommerce backend and rebuild important parts of the online store.

## Implementation order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Export and preserve Loyverse information">
    Export required products, inventory, customers, receipts, reports, staff configuration, and settings to protected business storage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the exact iMin model">
    Record the model, official Android version, available update, Google services, and built-in printer details without publishing serial numbers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test Shopify POS on the iMin">
    Test installation, sign-in, products, location inventory, printer, external payments, offline recovery, and a full store shift.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Purchase modular hardware when required">
    If the iMin or printer fails, procure the verified iPad, exact Epson printer, and secure stand after written quotations are approved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure payment types">
    Create the approved cash, card, and QR methods and document the separate Philippine terminal process.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test complete store transactions">
    Cover sales, discounts, cash, card, QR, refunds, returns, receipts, inventory movement, interruption, and reconnection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Train personnel">
    Train store staff, administrators, and the person responsible for daily payment reconciliation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm operational results">
    Verify inventory, receipts or invoices, refunds, payment settlements, permissions, and daily totals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stop new Loyverse transactions">
    Cut over only after the Shopify POS workflow is working and management approves the store date and support coverage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preserve the Loyverse export">
    Retain the approved export and access record for reference, audit, accounting, and support needs.
  </Step>
</Steps>
