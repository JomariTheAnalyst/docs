Application compatibility does not prove that Sixthgear can accept, reconcile, and document payments correctly. Payment and invoice review must be completed before Shopify POS becomes the live store system.

## Current payment limitation

Shopify's list of countries where Shopify Payments supports both online and in-person processing does not include the Philippines as of **July 16, 2026**. Sixthgear should therefore plan for a separate local card or QR terminal unless the selected provider and Shopify confirm an approved integration.

<Warning>
Do not purchase a Shopify card reader from another country. Availability, activation, currency, settlement, and support are country-specific, and no Shopify reader was verified for Philippine use in this research.
</Warning>

## External-terminal workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the sale in Shopify POS">
    Add the correct products, variants, quantities, discounts, taxes, and customer information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the amount on the external terminal">
    Enter the final amount and verify the currency and payment type before presenting the terminal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive payment">
    Wait for the terminal's approved confirmation. A customer screenshot or message is not payment confirmation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record the payment type in Shopify POS">
    Select the approved custom card or QR payment type and complete the Shopify sale only after the terminal succeeds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Match both records">
    Confirm that the Shopify order amount, terminal transaction, settlement report, and required invoice reference agree.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Payment types to test

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Cash">
    Test tendered amount, change, opening float, drawer movement, cash count, refund handling, and end-of-day differences.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Card">
    Test approved, declined, interrupted, duplicated, reversed, refunded, and partially refunded transactions. Confirm settlement timing and fees with the selected Philippine provider.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="QR payment">
    Test the provider's generated code or terminal flow, confirmation screen, duplicate prevention, refund path, settlement report, and the Shopify custom payment type.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Split or partial payment">
    Enable only when Sixthgear needs it and both the POS and external-terminal procedure can be reconciled without ambiguous balances.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Daily reconciliation

*Reconciliation* is the process of matching the POS, payment-provider, settlement, bank, and cash records.

| Record | Required check |
| --- | --- |
| Shopify POS sales | Completed orders match approved store transactions |
| Cash count | Physical cash matches expected drawer movement |
| Card and QR totals | POS payment types match terminal and settlement reports |
| Refunds and voids | Each has approval and the original transaction reference |
| Inventory movement | Completed sales and returns produced the expected stock change |
| Invoice sequence | Required documents are complete, traceable, and issued through the approved procedure |

Management must name the person who performs daily reconciliation, the person who investigates differences, and the person who approves adjustments.

## Philippine invoice and BIR review

Obtain qualified confirmation for required invoice details, sequential numbering, VAT and tax treatment, discounts, senior-citizen or PWD handling where applicable, refunds and credit documents, end-of-day reports, record retention, registration, and electronic invoicing.

<Note>
This page is operational planning, not legal, accounting, or tax advice. An accountant or BIR-qualified adviser must confirm the current requirements and the exact document Sixthgear will issue.
</Note>
