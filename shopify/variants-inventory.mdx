---
title: "Manage variants and inventory"
description: "Maintain product options, variant availability, SKUs, prices, and stock behavior used by the storefront."
icon: "boxes"
---

Variants represent customer choices such as size, color, or model. The storefront treats a product as available when at least one variant is available for sale.

## Before you edit variants

- Confirm the approved option names and values.
- Check whether apps, fulfillment systems, or imports depend on variant IDs or SKUs.
- Record existing inventory and price information before a large change.

<Warning>
Changing CSV option values can delete existing variant IDs and create new ones. That can break integrations that store Shopify variant IDs.
</Warning>

## Update a product

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the product">
    In Shopify Admin, open **Products**, then select the product.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review options">
    Confirm option names and values match the labels customers should see. Avoid duplicate values with different capitalization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review every variant">
    Check SKU, price, compare-at price when approved, inventory tracking, location assignment, and availability.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and test">
    Save the product. On the storefront, select each option combination and confirm price, image, stock message, and add-to-cart state.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Inventory rules to verify externally

The repository does not define Shopify locations, fulfillment services, overselling policy, stock alerts, or inventory ownership. Verify these settings in Shopify Admin before changing quantities or tracking behavior.
