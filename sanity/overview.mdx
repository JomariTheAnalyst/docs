SixthGear CMS is the Sanity Studio used to manage approved website content. Products, prices, inventory, customers, orders, checkout, and discounts remain in Shopify.

## Open SixthGear CMS

Use the environment that matches your task.

<CodeGroup>

```text Local development
http://localhost:7000/studio
```

```text Production (live)
https://www.sixthgearmoto.com/studio
```

</CodeGroup>

| Environment | Use it for | Important rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Local development | Developer testing against the locally running storefront | The storefront must already be running on port `7000`. Local changes do not update the live website. |
| Production | Approved content editing for the live SixthGear website | Confirm the account, document, and requested change before publishing. |

## What belongs in the CMS

SixthGear CMS manages editorial website content such as homepage sections, page copy, service content, Rider Stories, marketing content, and collection presentation content when those sections are confirmed as connected.

<Warning>
  Strapi still supplies content on active routes. Confirm a field's source before editing or migrating it.
</Warning>

## What belongs in Shopify

Use Shopify for:

- Product titles and descriptions
- Product media and metafields
- Variants, SKUs, prices, and inventory
- Collections and sales-channel availability
- Customers, orders, discounts, checkout, and payment-related records

Do not duplicate Shopify commerce data in Sanity unless a documented CMS field is specifically intended for editorial presentation.

## Main areas in Studio

The exact navigation depends on the current schema and the user’s assigned role.

| Area | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Homepage Settings** | Homepage editorial sections that have been connected and verified |
| **Marketing** | Announcement bars, popups, featured collections, and promo content under review |
| **Services Page** | Services landing-page content |
| **About Page** | About-page content, including **What We Offer** |
| **Services** | Individual service pages and their page content |
| **Blog** | Rider Story articles and categories |
| **Collection Hero** | Editorial presentation content for selected Shopify collections |

## Before editing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm your access">
    Sign in with the account approved for your role. Do not use another employee’s account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check content ownership">
    Confirm that the requested information belongs in Sanity rather than Shopify or storefront code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the coverage status">
    Continue with routine editing only when the section is marked **Ready**. An **Under review** section requires administrator approval and direct verification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the correct document">
    Confirm the document title and section before changing any field. Homepage work must use the intended **Homepage Settings** document.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish and verify">
    After publishing, open the affected live page and confirm the result on desktop and mobile.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Drafts, publishing, and live content

A draft is an unpublished version of a Sanity document. Selecting **Publish** makes the current document version available to the storefront, but caching, fallback logic, or an incorrect document connection can prevent the expected change from appearing immediately.

Do not publish a test change only to prove access. Open the document and review the available controls instead.

## Current operating limits

The CMS remains usable for sections marked **Ready**, but the documentation does not yet claim complete Sanity operations because:

- Sanity Visual Editing still requires final live-update verification.
- The canonical homepage document and duplicate homepage records still require final confirmation.
- Some page, marketing, article, media, and collection workflows remain under review.
