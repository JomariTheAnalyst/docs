Sanity validation protects editor input, but the storefront also depends on exact field names and GROQ projections. A schema-only change can leave published content unreadable.

## Field contract

| Layer | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| `sanity/schemaTypes/*.ts` | Field name, title, type, options, initial value and validation |
| `src/lib/cms/queries.ts` | GROQ projection and asset/reference resolution |
| `src/lib/cms/types.ts` | TypeScript shape used by the app |
| Components and pages | Rendering, fallback and conditional behavior |

## Common validation patterns

The current schemas use required strings, slugs, URLs, images, arrays, numeric ranges, and date comparisons. Marketing content also depends on active flags, positions, schedules, and reference targets.

## Change a field safely

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find every consumer">
    Search the schema name and field name across `sanity/`, `src/lib/cms/`, pages, and components.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Plan existing-content handling">
    Decide how old documents behave before you make a field required, rename it, or change its type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the complete contract">
    Change the schema, GROQ query, TypeScript type, fallback, and UI together.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test drafts and published content">
    Check Studio validation, publish behavior, frontend rendering, missing values, and existing documents.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Do not rename or delete a published field until existing documents are migrated and all frontend consumers are updated.
</Warning>
