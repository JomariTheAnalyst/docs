The production domain, registrar, and authoritative DNS provider require external verification. Source code and test configuration are not authoritative ownership records.

## Required records

Maintain the domain name, registrar, registrant organization, renewal date, auto-renew status, billing owner, recovery owner, nameservers, DNS provider, Vercel domain connection, and email-related DNS records in the internal register.

## Change DNS safely

<Steps>
  <Step title="Capture the current state">
    Export or record a redacted known-good zone and current nameservers. Confirm who approved the change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assess dependencies">
    Check website, email, verification, certificate, analytics, and third-party records that could be affected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make the smallest change">
    Use the authoritative provider. Avoid unrelated edits, removing the domain lock, or transfers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify propagation">
    Check authoritative and public DNS, HTTPS, redirects, Vercel domain status, and business email from more than one network.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record and monitor">
    Record the non-secret old and new records, TTL, approver, time, verification, and rollback condition.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Never remove or replace email-related DNS records while fixing the website unless the email owner has reviewed the impact.
</Warning>
