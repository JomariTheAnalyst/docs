---
title: "Emergency recovery"
description: "Contain incidents, restore service, recover access, and preserve evidence across SixthGear systems."
icon: "life-buoy"
---

Use the least destructive action first. Assign an incident lead and recorder once the responsible owners are known.

## Website unavailable

- **Symptoms:** Timeouts, error responses, incorrect redirects, or unexpected maintenance.
- **Initial checks:** Vercel deployment and logs, DNS, `/api/health`, Shopify, CMS, Redis, and a second network.
- **Containment:** Pause releases and preserve evidence.
- **Recovery:** Restore the known-good deployment or failed dependency.
- **Verify:** Homepage, store, product, cart, checkout, CMS content, forms, and smoke tests.

## Strapi content unavailable

- **Symptoms:** Missing services, navigation, preview, rider stories, coffee, sitemap, or homepage fallback content.
- **Initial checks:** Active import path, Strapi host status, URL reachability, and authentication class.
- **Containment:** Do not remove Strapi or overwrite content during the incident.
- **Recovery:** Restore hosting/API access or use an already implemented approved fallback.
- **Verify:** Every affected route family and content owner approval.

## Redis unavailable

- **Symptoms:** Cache errors, slow pages, repeated upstream calls, or invalidation failures.
- **Initial checks:** Upstash status, limits, logs, and affected key classes.
- **Containment:** Avoid a broad purge and monitor Shopify/Sanity load.
- **Recovery:** Restore Upstash configuration or service. The cache helper should fall back to the original fetcher.
- **Verify:** Product, collection, search, homepage, webhook behavior, and latency.

## Domain or DNS failure

- **Symptoms:** Resolution errors, certificate failures, wrong destination, or only a Vercel URL works.
- **Initial checks:** Registrar, nameservers, authoritative DNS, Vercel domain status, certificate, and recent changes.
- **Containment:** Freeze edits; keep the domain locked and do not transfer it.
- **Recovery:** Restore the verified known-good records through approved change control.
- **Verify:** Web, HTTPS, redirects, email records, and multiple networks.

## Suspected credential exposure

<Steps>
  <Step title="Restrict the exposure">
    Remove access to the exposed artifact and preserve necessary evidence without reposting the secret.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Revoke or rotate">
    Use the authoritative platform. Terminate unauthorized sessions and issue a least-privilege replacement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update environments">
    Update approved scopes and the password-manager item. Redeploy or restart only when required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify containment">
    Confirm the old credential fails, the integration works, and audit logs show no continuing misuse.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Handle source history">
    If Git contains the secret, use an approved history-cleaning incident process. Deleting the visible line is not enough.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recover platform access

For Shopify, Sanity, GitHub, Vercel, registrar, DNS, business email, password manager, and Mintlify, verify the owner, independent recovery route, MFA, support entitlement, and last recovery test.

<Warning>
Actual recovery codes, backup keys, passwords, identity documents, and private support correspondence must stay outside SixthGear Docs.
</Warning>

## Incident record

Record start and end time, systems, symptoms, deployment, external status, containment, recovery, approvals, impact, verifier, and follow-up owner. Exclude secret, customer, order, and payment data.
